Sending Stuff Into Space. Since 1958.

 

 

 

The Space Program was founded in 1958 with the goal of shooting as many things into space as possible. It all started with some unmanned satellites, but soon we were sending all sorts of things up there - monkeys, dogs, and eventually even people.
 
In the late 1960s, The Space Program accomplished its crowning moment by landing a man on the moon. At that point, it was imagined that soon, travel to other planets, and even other solar systems would be happening in just a few short years, but we were mistaken.
 
Since then, The Space Program has sent many unmanned vessels all over the galaxy, and established a space station orbiting the earth. We've landed robots on Mars, and used telescopes to peer into distant galaxies.
 
What happens next? In the next ten years we hope to build a moon base and begin plans to send men to Mars. We would say that the sky is the limit, but we've gone far beyond the sky.
